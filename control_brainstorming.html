Brainstorming Workshop Plan

Introduction (5 min): We will start with team introductions.

Icebreaker activity/warm-up (5 min): A quick warm-up exercise to get everyone thinking about AI.

Instructions for Miro (5 min): We will quickly go over the guidelines for our brainstorming session and show you how to use Miro.

Open brainstorming (40 min): Your team will brainstorm anything related to the AI use case - benefits, risks, mitigations, or neutral observations. Think out of the box! Use colored sticky notes: red for risks, green for benefits, blue for mitigations, grey for neutral.

Group and consolidate ideas (35 min): Organize your ideas by grouping similar notes, expanding on them, and adding new ideas as needed.

Workshop conclusion (5 min): We will wrap up with key takeaways and reflections on the process.

Complete Post-study Questionnaire (10 min): Finally, you will each complete a post-study questionnaire.

Brainstorming Guidelines

  • Nominate a Coordinator: Choose one person to add your team's ideas to the Miro board.
  • Think Broadly: Brainstorm anything related to the AI use case - benefits, risks, mitigations, or neutral observations.
  • Use Color Coding: Red sticky notes for risks, green for benefits, blue for mitigations, grey for neutral observations.
  • Welcome Wild Ideas: Encourage creative and unusual suggestions. Feel free to think out-of-the-box and consider both common and rare ideas.
  • Build on Others' Ideas: Listen to your teammates and think about how you can combine, improve, or extend their ideas.
  • Stay Focused: All ideas must be plausible and directly related to the AI use case.
